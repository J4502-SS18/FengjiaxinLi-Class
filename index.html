Drift in Columbia

Nov. 2, 2017

Sitting on the bench or standing at the crossing, they hold a sign writing “homeless” or “need help”, even “God bless you”.

They are strangers, but you are familiar with them. Homeless, a group of people are wandering from place to place in the city with a similar sign and different stories.


Rick is the member of this minor group. He has been roaming in Columbia for several years. In a gradually colder winter, they still can’t find their homes.

Rick is sitting on the 9th Street.

Rick calls himself John Doe, which is a name from those who are unidentified when dead.

He refuses to tell his full name. He sits on the Eighth Street, holding a sign, embedded by his coat from the charity.

He was born in California in 1984 and grew up as a foster child. He traveled from a family to another family just like he travels from one place to another.

In his 21, he came to Kansas City. He used to have a job in Columbia, but he was fired. Now, it is the fourth year he has been in this city.

“I’m not homeless, I’m houseless,” he said, “when I apply for a job, they need me to fill the application, which needs my address. And I don’t have it.”
